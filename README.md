# Bank-Loan-Analysis

##### An end-to-end Bank Loan Analysis project built with Excel, SQL, and Tableau to evaluate loan portfolio performance, repayment trends, borrower profiles, and credit risk through interactive dashboards and business intelligence reporting.

🛠 Tech Stack

   Tableau – Interactive dashboard development and data visualization
   SQL (MySQL) – Data extraction, querying, aggregation, and analysis
   Microsoft Excel – Data source, cleaning, and preprocessing
   Data Modeling – Data relationships and business logic implementation
   Calculated Fields – Custom metrics and KPI calculations in Tableau

✨ Features
  Executive dashboard with key loan performance KPIs
  Interactive filters for Year, Loan Status, and Loan Grade
  Year-over-Year loan trend analysis
  State-wise loan distribution analysis
  Good Loan vs Bad Loan performance tracking
  Grade and Sub-grade revolving balance analysis
  Verified vs Non-Verified borrower comparison
  Home ownership-based payment analysis
  Dynamic and interactive charts for business exploration
  

📌 Business Problem
    Banks generate large volumes of loan data, making it difficult to monitor portfolio performance, repayment behavior, and credit risk using traditional reports. Decision-makers need an interactive dashboard to evaluate loan quality, identify high-risk segments, and optimize lending strategies.

🎯 Goal of the Dashboard
    -Analyze the overall performance of the bank's loan portfolio.
    -Monitor loan funding, repayments, and revenue generation.
    -Evaluate Good Loan and Bad Loan percentages.
    -Identify high-performing and high-risk customer segments.
    -Support data-driven lending and credit risk management.
    
💼 Business Impact
    -Improved visibility into loan portfolio performance through a centralized dashboard.
    -Enabled faster identification of high-risk loans and default-prone segments.
    -Helped management monitor repayment trends and lending performance.
    -Supported credit risk assessment using loan grades and borrower verification.
    -Reduced manual reporting effort by providing interactive self-service analytics.
    -Enhanced strategic decision-making with real-time loan portfolio insights.
    
📈 Key Business Insights
  The loan portfolio maintained a Good Loan Rate of 82.96%, indicating strong overall portfolio health.
  Loan issuance showed consistent growth between 2007 and 2011, reflecting increased lending activity.
  California recorded the highest number of loan accounts, making it the largest lending market.
  Loan Grades A and B contributed the highest revolving balances, indicating better-performing borrower segments.
  Verified borrowers generated higher total repayments compared to non-verified borrowers, emphasizing the value of customer verification.
  Mortgage holders accounted for the largest share of last payment amounts, followed by renters and homeowners.
  Interactive filtering allows stakeholders to analyze loan performance by year, status, and grade for better credit risk assessment.
